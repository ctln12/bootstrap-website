One cactus, many cacti or cactuses, or less commonly, many cactus. Cacti occur in a wide range of shapes and sizes. Most cacti live in habitats subject to at least some drought. Many live in extremely dry environments. Cacti show many adaptations to conserve water.

Cultivation

Cacti naturally occur in a wide range of habitats and are then grown in many countries with different climates, so precisely replicating the conditions in which a species normally grows is usually not practical.

Growing medium

An open medium with a high air content is important such as 25–75% organic-based material and inorganic material for the rest.

Watering

Semi-desert cacti need careful watering. During the growing season, cacti should be allowed to dry out between thorough waterings.

Light and Temperature

Cacti can be exposed to high light levels but need shading if grown in a greenhouse in summer. Suggested temperature: 5 °C - 10 °C.

Growth Habit

Arborescent Cacti

They can be tree-like (arborescent), meaning they typically have a single more-or-less woody trunk topped by several to many branches.

> Opuntia

Columnar Cacti

Smaller cacti may be described as columnar. They consist of erect, cylinder-shaped stems, which may or may not branch, without a very clear division into trunk and branches.

> Old Man Cactus

Globular Cacti

Cacti whose stems are even smaller may be described as globular (or globose). They consist of shorter, more ball-shaped stems than columnar cacti.

> Ferocactus

Other Cacti

Other cacti have a quite different appearance.

> Epiphyte
